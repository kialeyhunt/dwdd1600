Synopsis

On a faraway world, dragons rule the land. Ten empires stretch across the continent known as Zurra. For over three millennia, these ten races coexisted in periods of both peace and discord.

Time has now ebbed into a new era of war, for three of the ten races have effectively vanished. With the rise of this new conflict comes seven dragons destined to find the missing souls and bring balance back to Zurra – but is the prophecy and their powers going to be enough?

Meet the Empires

CloudWalkers
home: The Floating City
appearance: smallest dragon of all species, in both height and weight; smooth scales with oversized bumps/ridges along back and belly; circular scale pattern lines body all the way to tail; scales can be paler and pastel shades of white, light gray, blue, or soft green
abilities: mist breath (effects cause drowsiness and sleep); the only species with the ability to walk on clouds; incredible sense of smell; nimble talons capable of handling small, delicate things that most dragons cannot
FireTeeth
home: Magma Mountain
appearance: thick black scales coat the outermost layer of the body; underbelly and underwings glow softly; compact wings to fit in caves and tighter spaces; scales can be any shade of red, orange, or yellow with lighter colored underbelly
abilities: red-hot liquid lava breath; breathing in smoke does not harm or impair extra tough lungs; internal organs burs just as fiery as a volcano, which stabilizes the body and allows it to endure unimaginable degrees of heat, to the point where it is questioned if there is even a limit; eyes are used to both bright lights and seeing in dim caves: extra eyelid closes to protect eyes when swimming and in bright light, while pupils enlarge to an incredible size when in the dark; bottom of talons have pads that can adhere to any surface, used for scaling rock walls of inner volcano
PrairieDiggers
home: Heather Hills
appearance: black and white markings similar to that of a pronghorn; outward-facing horns on the end of snout; long black spines trailing from head to tail; smooth body with ridged underbelly; large ears for hearing over long distances; scales can range from medium brown to pale yellow
abilities: breath helps increase and expedite plant growth; plow ground with nasal horns; strong limbs for hard work; badger-like claws for digging
SandClaws
home: Sallara Sands
appearance: long body and smooth underbelly like a snake; small but sharp ridges trail the spine, as well as guard each leg and wing; head is protected with helmet-like armor; end of long tail is green and spiked, like a cactus; scales can be dusky brown, beige, or sandy yellow
abilities: fire breath; can withstand immense heat from the sun; bottoms of feet are covered in special pads to walk on blistering sand, with longer claws made for burrowing; cactus-like tail is most valuable natural weapon; can go long periods of time without eating or drinking (extra fat is stored in tail, similar to a camel’s hump)
SnowScales
home: Lavinu (The Greater Empire), Vibralt Peninsula (The Lesser Empire)
appearance: body made up of rigid and sharp scales; extra thick, armor-like scales found on top of head, shoulders, and legs; strong underbelly scales for sliding across snow and ice; skull and jaw are equipped with eight total horns/spikes; large, thick ridges trail down spine; heavily spiked tail tip; palms of feet are serrated to better grip the ice; scales can be pale shades of white, blue, or gray
abilities: ice shard breath; can withstand sub-zero temperatures; hold breath for long periods of time to dive underwater for prey; strong wings for flying through blizzards; armor-like scales protect skin from being pierced by hail and shards of ice; blubbery black skin underneath scales keep body heat trapped for long periods of time and can store energy gained from food; spiked tail can be used to break ice to create fishing holes, dens, or plow a path (may also be used to kill prey); can easily camouflage into the arctic environment; warm-blooded, so as to withstand the extreme frigid temperatures
StarSeekers
home: The Hum
appearance: compact bodies encrusted with glow-in-the-dark scales and armored with thick plates; long, sharp spines; small talons capable of handling fragile items; tall, curving horns sprouting from skull; black, gray, blue, green, or purple scales with brightly colored highlights
abilities: molten gold breath; can illuminate scales to shine in the dark; able to see in almost pure blackness; able to hold breath for over an hour, and store oxygen in pouches hidden under scale-helmet to allow for day-long journeys outside of the Hum
StormFliers
home: The Forsaken Mountains
appearance: sleek bodies to repel the rain during storms; small spines for more aerodynamic shape; horns are shaped like bolts of lightning; small, narrow snouts for reaching into small crevices to catch prey; droplet-shaped scales armor legs and wings; scales range from shades of gray, blue, and black and can be known to have yellow or tan markings
abilities: electric breath; able to fly through the thickest and toughest of storms with impossibly strong wings; tough scales and muscles are able to absorb shocks from lightning; roars can be heard from miles away and resemble thunder; able to scale mountain walls with tough talons and claws; protective nictating eyelid for flying in the rain; piercing eyesight akin to an eagle’s to see prey far away and in bad weather; some of the strongest fliers, rivaled only by WindGliders
TreeWatchers
home: Daiyla Woods
appearance: head is covered in feathers down to end of neck; can have any type of antlers or horns; oversized ears for hearing through dense flora; long claws for digging and scaling trees; scales be any earthy color, such as green, brown, beige, gray, etc.; feathers and underwing can be any color
abilities: healing/restoring breath (can help promote plant growth, heal almost any animal wound or plant breakage, can help mend smaller and less serious dragon wounds depending on age and expertise of dragon); can communicate to certain forest animals; body only needs bare minimum of nutrients/ food to sustain itself (can survive on roots and plant shoots); incredible sense of smell and hearing; long, nimble limbs used for climbing and navigating through thick trees; smaller torso and wings, so as to fit through tight spaces and not get caught on branches
WaterTails
home: The Sea Scales
appearance: large dorsal fin on spine, fin on stomach, and two more on tail; tail is large and shaped like a marlin’s; small, bony spines trail down back and sides; bioluminescent antenna hangs from skull; webbed talons; scales can be any color, as ranging as the fish of the sea
abilities: breathe out boiling water; two sets of lungs: gills on side of neck allow them to be able to breathe underwater; webbed talons, wings, and tail are used to make them adept and powerful swimmers; can see in the dark for diving deep underwater; antennae on the top of skull uses bioluminescence to glow and aid in hunting; can smell blood up to a mile away with nares in snout
